In Japanese culture, Omotenashi meaning hospitality, is very significant. Omotenashi is a way of living in Japan, and focuses on providing the best possible services when it comes to hospitality, while also expecting nothing in return. This meaningful concept is what inspired our cafe name. We would like to give our customers the same superb hospitality through omotenashi.

The doodles in which you see decorating our website are called Seigaiha waves. Seigaiha literally means ‘blue sea and waves’. Seigaiha waves are a traditional Japanese design which symbolize power and resistance - key elements of Japanese culture.

Polite Courtesies

As part of Japanese culture, chefs and staff usually shout "IRASSHAIMASE!" altogether whenever a customer enters their café to give them a warm welcome.

In Japan, customers often slurp soup and drinks to let the chef know it tastes delicious. Always treating customers with kindness is always an important value to have in Japanese restaurants and cafés.

Cleanliness

Keeping areas clean at all times is also important for Japanese Cafés as one of Japan's main religions, Shintoism, conducts the rituals of purity and purification frequently
